## 📖 Project Overview
This 2D motion graphics project tells the emotional and thought-provoking story of an Egyptian Pound coin. Starting as a shiny, new coin, it is passed around until it becomes rusty, dirty, and rejected by society in favor of a modern, shiny Visa card. Facing deep depression and a sense of worthlessness, the coin's journey takes a dramatic turn when it is donated to a mosque. There, it unites with thousands of other "forgotten" and rusty coins to purchase a life-saving medical ventilator for a hospital. The project highlights the contrast between superficial appearances (the Visa card as mere "numbers in the air") and true, tangible value (building reality and saving human lives).

---

## 🎯 Project Objectives
- To deliver a strong moral and social message about true value versus superficial appearances.
- To apply advanced 2D motion graphics, character rigging, and animation techniques effectively.
- To create engaging character acting (facial expressions and body language) for inanimate objects (a coin and a credit card).
- To successfully manage a complete animation pipeline from scriptwriting to final rendering through teamwork and collaboration.

---

## 🛠️ Project Scope
- **Scriptwriting & Storyboarding:** Visualizing the 4 main scenes and planning the camera shots.
- **Character Design & Rigging:** Creating the Pound and Visa card characters in a flat vector style with bold outlines, and preparing them for animation.
- **Background & Environment Design:** Drawing the distinct environments (street, microbus, pocket, mosque, hospital) in Adobe Illustrator.
- **2D Animation:** Animating character movements, transitions, and the emotional shifts across all scenes in Adobe After Effects.
- **Sound Design & Voiceover:** Adding background music, sound effects (SFX), and the final dialogue between the characters.

> **Final Deliverable:** A fully animated, high-quality 2D motion graphics short film, ready for presentation and portfolio showcasing.

---

## 📅 Project Plan (5 Weeks)

### Week 1 — Pre-production (Story & Storyboard)
- Finalize the script, sketch the storyboard for the 4 main scenes, and gather visual references for the flat vector art style.

### Week 2 — Asset Design (Characters & Backgrounds)
- Design the main characters (the new/rusty Pound and the Visa card) and draw all environment backgrounds using Adobe Illustrator.

### Week 3 — Rigging & Scene Setup
- Import all vector assets into Adobe After Effects, rig the characters' limbs and facial expressions, and set up the composition for each scene.

### Week 4 — Animation & Motion
- Animate character movements, emotional reactions, and scene transitions (from the microbus to the pocket, and finally to the hospital).

### Week 5 — Post-production (Audio & Final Render)
- Add sound effects, background music, and the final dialogue recording. Render the final video and prepare the presentation file for the final evaluation.
